
# Spatiotemporal Phenology & NDVI Analysis: Achimota Forest, Accra (2024)

## Project Overview
This repository presents a comprehensive 12-month analysis of the plant life cycle in the **Achimota Forest Reserve** for the year 2024. Using **Normalized Difference Vegetation Index (NDVI)** as a proxy for photosynthetic activity, this study tracks the phenological transitions—leafing, flowering, fruiting, and senescence—of one of Ghana's most critical urban tropical forests.

## The Ecological Significance of Achimota Forest
Located in the Okaikwei North Municipality of Accra, the Achimota Forest is a vital ecological lifeline for the city. As one of the last significant green spaces in the metropolitan area, it serves several key functions:
*   **Microclimate Regulation**: Mitigating the Urban Heat Island (UHI) effect in Accra through evapotranspiration.
*   **Biodiversity Hotspot**: Supporting local flora and fauna within a dense urban landscape.
*   **Social & Cultural Value**: Providing essential recreational space and cultural heritage for the capital's residents.

---

## Methodology: Tracking the Plant Life Cycle
The plant life cycle (phenology) in tropical urban forests is primarily driven by seasonal climatic variations, specifically rainfall and temperature. This study utilized **NDVI** to monitor these stages:

1.  **Senescence & Dormancy (January – March)**: Lower NDVI values reflect the dry season's impact, showing reduced photosynthetic activity.
2.  **Greening & Leafing (April – June)**: Increasing NDVI values coincide with the onset of the raining season, signaling new growth.
3.  **Peak Vigor (September)**: Reaching the highest vegetation productivity of the year.
4.  **Transition to Senescence (October – December)**: A gradual decline as the forest prepares for the drier harmattan period.

---

## Key Findings

### 1. Annual Peak and Trough
*   **Peak Productivity (September):** Vegetation vigor reached its maximum in September, characterized by deep green values across the forest core. This reflects optimal health and maximum photosynthetic capacity.
*   **Annual Low (February):** The lowest NDVI values were recorded in February, representing the toughest period for the forest's plant life due to seasonal water scarcity.

### 2. Data Quality Advisory
> [!CAUTION]
> **July 2024 Data Anomaly:** Due to significant sensor interference or cloud cover inconsistencies, the NDVI map for July appears distorted. Researchers and planners should interpret results for this specific month with caution, as the artifacts do not represent actual vegetation health.

## Conclusion
The 2024 analysis illustrates a direct correlation between the forest's life cycle and seasonal climatic patterns. The Achimota Forest remains a dynamic and resilient urban ecosystem, but its health is deeply sensitive to the timing and intensity of Accra's rainfall cycles. This data provides a baseline for ongoing conservation efforts and urban forest management.
