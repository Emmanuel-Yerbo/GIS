
# Understanding River Networks: The Strahler Way
## Watershed Analysis of Prestea Huni Valley Municipality, Ghana

## Project Overview
Understanding river networks is critical for the effective management of water resources and the mitigation of hydrological risks. This project applies the **Strahler Stream Order** classification—a numerical ranking system—to the drainage network of the **Prestea Huni Valley Municipality** in the Western Region of Ghana. By delineating stream orders, this study provides a foundational framework for watershed management and sustainable land-use planning.

## Methodology: The Strahler Stream Ordering System
The Strahler method is a hierarchical approach used to rank stream segments based on their level of branching within a drainage network:
*   **First-Order Streams (1)**: The smallest, unbranched headwater tributaries.
*   **Second-Order Streams (2)**: Formed when two first-order streams converge.
*   **Third-Order Streams (3)**: Formed when two second-order streams join.
*   **Fourth-Order Streams (4)**: Formed by the joining of two third-order streams.

> [!NOTE]
> When streams of different orders meet, the downstream segment retains the higher order of the two (e.g., a 2nd-order stream meeting a 3rd-order stream remains a 3rd-order segment).

## Data & Study Area
*   **Location**: Prestea Huni Valley Municipality, Western Region, Ghana.
*   **Data Source**: **Shuttle Radar Topography Mission (SRTM)** Digital Elevation Model (DEM) with a **30m resolution**.
*   **Analysis**: The DEM was used to delineate the terrain and accurately trace the flow paths creating the municipality's hydrological network.

---

## Key Hydro-Geomorphic Findings
The identification of a **fourth-order stream** within the Prestea Huni Valley indicates a **well-developed and intricate drainage system**. 

### Significance of a 4th-Order Stream:
*   **Intricate Hydrology**: Reflects a complex network of multiple tributary junctions.
*   **Water Availability**: Represents a major hydrological framework that is vital for maintaining local water supply.
*   **Ecosystem & Agriculture**: Supports diverse ecosystems and provides the primary water source for the region's agricultural productivity.

---

## Conclusion & Applications
Classifying stream orders is more than a mapping exercise; it is an essential tool for **Watershed Management**. The results of this study can be used to:
1.  **Predict Hydrological Behavior**: Assessing flood risks and sediment transport patterns.
2.  **Guide Sustainable Land Use**: Identifying sensitive drainage zones for environmental protection.
3.  **Optimize Resource Allocation**: Strategic planning for water extraction and irrigation.
